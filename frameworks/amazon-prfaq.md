# Amazon PR/FAQ Framework

Work backwards from the customer experience to define the product.

## Core Concept

Write the press release BEFORE building the product. If you can't write a compelling press release, you don't have a compelling product. The FAQ section forces you to address hard questions early.

## Structure

### Press Release (1 page max)

```markdown
## {Product Name} — {Subtitle that explains what it does}

**{City, Date}** — {Company} today announced {product name}, a {category}
that enables {target customer} to {key benefit}.

{Paragraph 2: The problem. What's broken today. Why existing solutions fail.}

{Paragraph 3: The solution. How this product works. What makes it different.}

"{Customer quote — a fictional customer describing how this changed their work/life},"
said {fictional customer name}, {title} at {company}.

{Paragraph 4: How to get started. Call to action.}

"{Internal quote — why this matters to the company},"
said {leader name}, {title}.

For more information, visit {URL}.
```

### FAQ Section

#### External FAQ (Customer Questions)
1. What does this product do?
2. Who is this for?
3. How is this different from {competitor}?
4. How much does it cost?
5. How do I get started?
6. What happens if {common concern}?

#### Internal FAQ (Stakeholder Questions)
1. Why should we build this?
2. What's the market size?
3. What resources do we need?
4. What are the biggest risks?
5. How does this align with our strategy?
6. What's the timeline?
7. How will we measure success?
8. What happens if we don't build this?

## Why It Works

1. **Forces clarity**: You can't hide behind vague specs in a press release
2. **Customer-first thinking**: Starts with the experience, not the technology
3. **Kills bad ideas fast**: If the press release isn't compelling, the product won't be either
4. **Alignment tool**: Everyone reads the same document and envisions the same product
5. **FAQ surfaces risks**: Hard questions are answered before investment, not after

## When to Use in Prisma PM

- During `/pm:define` with `--format full` — as an alternative framing exercise
- Before major investments — as a gut-check on product value
- For stakeholder communication — as an alignment artifact

## Tips

- If you struggle to write the customer quote, your product isn't solving a real problem
- The internal FAQ is where hard truths live — don't skip it
- Keep iterating the press release until it's genuinely exciting to read
- The press release should be understandable by anyone, not just domain experts
