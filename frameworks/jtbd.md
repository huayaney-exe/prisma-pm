# Jobs-to-be-Done (JTBD) Framework

People don't buy products — they hire them to get a job done.

## Core Concept

A "job" is the progress a person is trying to make in a particular circumstance. Products are hired to make that progress happen. When a product does the job well, it gets re-hired. When it doesn't, it gets fired.

## Three Types of Jobs

### Functional Job
What the user needs to accomplish.
> "When I'm running late for a meeting across town, I need to get there in 15 minutes."

### Emotional Job
How the user wants to feel.
> "I want to feel in control and not anxious about being late."

### Social Job
How the user wants to be perceived.
> "I want to be seen as reliable and professional by my colleagues."

## Job Statement Template

```
When {situation/circumstance},
I want to {action/capability},
So I can {expected outcome}.
```

**Key elements:**
- **Circumstance** is everything. The same person has different jobs in different contexts.
- **The job is stable** — people have needed transportation for centuries. Solutions change; jobs don't.
- **Competition is cross-category** — your competitors aren't just similar products. A job can be served by a spreadsheet, a phone call, or doing nothing.

## Hiring and Firing

### Hiring Criteria
What pushes someone to "hire" a new solution:
- **Push of the situation**: Current situation is getting worse
- **Pull of the new solution**: New option looks attractive
- **Anxiety of the new**: Fear of switching
- **Habit of the present**: Comfort with current approach

```
Hiring happens when: Push + Pull > Anxiety + Habit
```

### Firing Criteria
What causes someone to "fire" a solution:
- Doesn't get the core functional job done
- Creates new problems (emotional or social)
- Better alternative appears
- Circumstance changes (job no longer exists)

## Circumstance-Based Segmentation

Traditional segmentation: "Sarah, 34, Marketing Manager"
JTBD segmentation: "When a non-technical team lead needs to present data insights to executives..."

| Traditional | JTBD-Based |
|-------------|-----------|
| Age, gender, income | Circumstance, trigger, urgency |
| "Who they are" | "What they're trying to do" |
| Static | Dynamic |
| Correlative | Causal |

## Application in Prisma PM

- `/pm:discover` — Every discovery maps to functional, emotional, and social jobs
- `/pm:persona` — Personas defined by JTBD, not demographics
- `/pm:define` — PRDs reference the jobs being served
- `/pm:require` — Every requirement traces to a user job

## Interview Questions for JTBD

1. "Tell me about the last time you tried to {do the job}."
2. "What were you trying to accomplish?"
3. "What did you end up doing? Walk me through it step by step."
4. "What was frustrating about that approach?"
5. "What would have made it better?"
6. "Have you looked for other solutions? What happened?"
