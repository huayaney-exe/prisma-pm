# Product Management Anti-Patterns

Patterns that destroy product value. When you detect these in your work or in user behavior, flag them immediately.

## The Feature Factory

**Symptom**: Shipping features without connecting them to user transformation.
**Detection**: PRDs that list features but never mention the undesired state or preferred state.
**Fix**: Every feature must answer "what transformation does this enable?" If you can't answer, don't build it.

## Solution-First Discovery

**Symptom**: Jumping to solutions before understanding the problem.
**Detection**: User says "we need a dashboard" before articulating who needs it and why.
**Fix**: Force problem-space exploration first. Use the Socratic 5-layer questioning: Who? What state? Preferred state? Blockers? Frequency?

## Vanity Metrics

**Symptom**: Measuring activity instead of transformation.
**Detection**: Success metrics like "page views," "sign-ups," "DAU" without connecting to user outcomes.
**Fix**: Tie every metric to a Product Power Formula component. "Users who complete onboarding in <5 min" beats "onboarding page views."

## PRD Theater

**Symptom**: Writing PRDs that nobody reads because they lack context.
**Detection**: PRDs that are 20 pages of obvious requirements with no transformation framing.
**Fix**: Context-engineer every PRD. Include the "why" behind every decision. Make it self-sufficient for AI-assisted implementation.

## P1 Everything

**Symptom**: Refusing to force-rank priorities.
**Detection**: Backlog where 80% of items are "P1" or "High Priority."
**Fix**: Use Product Power Formula + RICE scoring. Force unique rankings. No ties allowed.

## Analysis Paralysis

**Symptom**: Endless discovery loops that never converge to action.
**Detection**: Multiple rounds of research with no hypothesis formulation or experiment design.
**Fix**: Apply the AI Compression Effect — if traditional discovery takes weeks, AI-native discovery takes hours. Set time boxes. Ship experiments, not research reports.

## Persona Fiction

**Symptom**: Creating demographic personas without behavioral grounding.
**Detection**: Personas defined by age, job title, and stock photo rather than by circumstance, job-to-be-done, and transformation sought.
**Fix**: Use JTBD-based personas. Define by circumstance and job, not demographics. "When [situation], I need [outcome]" beats "Sarah, 34, Marketing Manager."

## Management Theater

**Symptom**: Coordinating without creating value.
**Detection**: PM's calendar is 90% meetings, 0% customer interaction, 0% building.
**Fix**: Every PM activity must connect to one of the 15 JTBD. If it doesn't, it's theater. Cut it.

## Building for Everyone

**Symptom**: No ICP definition, trying to serve all possible users.
**Detection**: Product serves no one well because it was designed for everyone.
**Fix**: Define ICP with disqualification criteria. Knowing who you DON'T serve is as important as knowing who you do.

## Cargo Cult Agile

**Symptom**: Following agile rituals without understanding their purpose.
**Detection**: Sprint ceremonies happen but nothing ships. Stories are written but lack acceptance criteria. Retrospectives happen but nothing changes.
**Fix**: Every ceremony must produce an artifact that feeds the product lifecycle. No artifact = no meeting.
